Software as a service

About SaaS

Building a system that meets all your needs can work out very expensive, and time consuming. SaaS offers fully functional applications on-demand, providing greater flexibility, and typically you pay only for the number of users you need on a subscription basis, which can be scaled up and down based on your needs. SaaS offers a wide range of services such as email management, Customer Relationship Management, Case Management, and the list of applications keeps on growing.

With a SaaS, you are buying for the use of a providerâ€™s application, and the cloud infrastructure used to run it. Applications are typically accessible from various devices through a interfaces such as a web browser. You do not manage or control the underlying cloud infrastructure including network, servers, operating systems, storage, or even individual application capabilities, with the possible exception of limited user-specific application configuration settings.

What to consider when buying SaaS

Requirements

Consider all of your requirements when searching for a service, this should include technical, procurement and security.

Onboarding / offboarding

Consider the onboarding and offboarding costs along with the cost of the service. Suppliers must provide the costs for entry into the service, along with the exits costs to return your data in a usable format.

Integration / standards

Consider the the integration requirements for the service. Does it need to integrate with your current identity service, or existing systems? Does the service support Open Standards for data exchange?
